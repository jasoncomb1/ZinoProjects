My school project involved the design and implementation of a Wooden extension circuit capable of supplying power to various electronic devices. The primary objective was to create a reliable and efficient circuit that could handle different loads and provide a safe power source.

Challenges Faced

1. Load Handling

One of the main challenges we faced was determining how the circuit would handle varying loads. Different devices have different power requirements, and ensuring the circuit could adapt to these without causing issues was a significant concern.

2. Overheating

As we tested the circuit with different loads, we encountered issues related to overheating. It was crucial to find a balance between circuit performance and preventing components from becoming too hot, which could pose safety risks.

How We Overcame Challenges

1. Load Handling

To address the load handling challenge, we conducted an extensive research on power distribution and reconnected diffrent wires to be able to distribute current accordingly based on the connected devices. This involved the use of specialized components and careful calibration.

2. Overheating

To tackle the overheating issue, we redesigned the circuit layout, optimized component placement, and we changed the wires to a much stronger one for free flow of current to over come the issue.
